
# Violin & Cello Harmony Guide: "Visual Clef" System

This guide is optimized for your specific scoring practice where:
1. Both **Viulu** (Violin) and **Sello** (Cello) use Treble Clef.
2. The **Sello** staff sounds **one octave lower** than written.

### The Interval Reference Table

| Visual Interval (on your score) | Sounding Interval | Semitones (mod 12) | Harmonic Character | Drama & Usage |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Unison** | Octave | 0 | **Pure / Grounded** | Powerful for main themes. Adds "weight" to a melody. |
| **2nd** | 9th | 2 | **Sparkly Tension** | **Surprising.** Sounds like a "suspended" romantic breath. |
| **3rd** | 10th | 3 or 4 | **Rich Harmony** | The "Standard" for beauty. Sounds full and quartet-like. |
| **4th** | 11th (P4 + Oct) | 5 | **Stark / Glassy** | Modern and a bit "hollow." Great for mystery. |
| **5th** | 12th (P5 + Oct) | 7 | **Epic / Heroic** | **Cathedral Sound.** Very resonant and stable. |
| **6th** | 13th (6th + Oct) | 8 or 9 | **Lush / Romantic** | Your **Golden Interval.** Best for emotional flow. |
| **7th** | 14th (7th + Oct) | 10 or 11 | **Biting Drama** | High emotional ache. Best for "pulling" toward a resolution. |

---

### Dramaturgical Strategies

#### 1. The "Expansion" (The Reveal)
* **Action:** Start both instruments on a **Visual Unison** (sounding octave). Move them in opposite directions (Violin up, Cello down).
* **Result:** Creates a sense of "opening a curtain" or a sudden emotional realization.

#### 2. The "Shadow" Melody
* **Action:** Write your melody for the Violin, then duplicate it for the Cello a **Visual 6th** lower.
* **Result:** This is the most reliable way to get a "professional" cinematic string sound.

#### 3. The "9th" Surprise
* **Action:** Use a **Visual 2nd** (sounding 9th) on a strong beat, then resolve the Violin up or Cello down into a **Visual 3rd**.
* **Result:** Provides a modern, sophisticated "shimmer" that feels beautiful rather than harsh.

---

### Technical Constraints (The "Red Lines")
Keep these in mind for your visual staves:
* **Cello Floor:** Don't go below the **C** with two ledger lines below your Sello treble staff.
* **Violin Floor:** Don't go below the **G** below the Viulu staff.
* **The Sweet Spot:** For a "dramatic but beautiful" balance, try to keep the instruments between a **Visual 3rd and a Visual 6th** apart. If they get closer than a visual 2nd, the sound can get "muddy" in the low register.
